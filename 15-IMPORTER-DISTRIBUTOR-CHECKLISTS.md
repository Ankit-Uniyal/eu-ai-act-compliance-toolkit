# 15 -- Importer and Distributor Compliance Checklists

**EU AI Act Reference:** Articles 23-24
**Applies to:** Importers and Distributors of High-Risk AI Systems
**Last Updated:** April 2026

---

## Purpose

The EU AI Act distributes obligations across the entire AI supply chain. Importers and Distributors are not responsible for designing AI systems, but carry important verification obligations before making high-risk AI systems available on the EU market.

---

## Role Definitions

| Role | Definition | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Importer | EU-established entity placing on the EU market a high-risk AI system from a non-EU provider | EU company purchasing AI from US vendor and selling it in the EU |
| Distributor | Any entity in the supply chain (other than provider or importer) making AI available without modifying it | Resellers, software distributors, marketplace platforms |

> Caution: If an Importer or Distributor modifies a high-risk AI system or places their own trademark on it, they may become a new Provider with full provider obligations (Article 25). See 10-PROVIDER-DEPLOYER-RESPONSIBILITIES.md.

## PART C -- When Does an Importer or Distributor Become a Provider? (Article 25)

| Action | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Place own name or trademark on a high-risk AI system | Become provider -- full obligations apply |
| Make a substantial modification to a high-risk AI system | Become provider -- full obligations apply |
| Change intended purpose so system now qualifies as high-risk | Become provider -- full obligations apply |

Definition of Substantial Modification (Article 3(23)): A change to an AI system after placement on market that affects the system's compliance with requirements or its intended purpose.

If any of the above applies, consult 10-PROVIDER-DEPLOYER-RESPONSIBILITIES.md and complete the full provider obligations.
