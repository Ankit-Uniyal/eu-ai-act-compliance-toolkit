# 10 — Provider & Deployer Responsibilities Matrix

**EU AI Act Reference:** Articles 16–27 | Articles 49, 72, 73  
**Applies to:** Providers, Deployers, Importers, Distributors  
**Last Updated:** April 2026

---

## Purpose

The EU AI Act distributes responsibilities across the AI value chain. This document clarifies who must do what, organised by role and by obligation area, to prevent compliance gaps in organisations that both develop and deploy AI systems.

---

## Part 1 — Role Definitions

| Role | Definition | Typical Examples |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Provider** | Places AI system on market or puts it into service; develops AI for own use | AI vendors, SaaS companies, internal AI teams building for org use |
| **Deployer** | Uses AI system in professional context, not the provider | Enterprise customers, public authorities using third-party AI |
| **Importer** | Places AI system from outside EU on the EU market | Distributors of non-EU AI products |
| **Distributor** | Makes AI system available on market without modifying it | Resellers, app stores |
| **Authorised Representative** | EU-established entity acting on behalf of non-EU provider | EU legal entity designated by non-EU provider |

**Important:** An organisation can be **both** a provider AND a deployer for different systems. Some organisations that modify a third-party AI system significantly may become the **new provider** under Article 25.

## Part 3 — When Does a Deployer Become a Provider? (Article 25)

A deployer becomes the new **provider** — with full provider obligations — if they:

| Action | Outcome |
|--------|---------|
| Place their name/trademark on the system | Become provider |
| Make a substantial modification to a high-risk system | Become provider |
| Change the intended purpose such that it becomes high-risk | Become provider |
| Develop an AI system for putting into service under their own name | Provider from the start |

**Definition of Substantial Modification (Article 3(23)):**
> A change to an AI system after it has been placed on the market that affects the system's compliance with requirements or its intended purpose.

## Part 4 — Obligation Allocation for Third-Party AI Systems

Use this table when your organisation deploys a third-party AI system:

| Obligation Area | Who Does It? | Evidence Required |
|----------------|-------------|------------------|
| Conformity assessment | **Provider** | Certificate / DoC from provider |
| Technical documentation | **Provider** | Request from provider |
| EU database registration (system) | **Provider** | Registration number from provider |
| EU database registration (deployer — public sector) | **Deployer** | Self-registered |
| Instructions for use | **Provider** supplies; **Deployer** follows | Instructions document |
| Human oversight design | **Provider** designs; **Deployer** implements | Oversight plan |
| Human oversight operation | **Deployer** | Oversight logs |
| FRIA | **Deployer** (if applicable) | FRIA document |
| Incident reporting to provider | **Deployer** | Incident log |
| Incident reporting to MSA | **Provider** (or Deployer if provider not in EU) | Notification record |
| Post-market monitoring | **Provider** runs; **Deployer** contributes data | PMM plan + reports |
| Log retention | **Deployer** retains logs they control | Log archive |
