# 28 — Market Surveillance and Regulatory Response Procedure
## EU AI Act Compliance Toolkit | v3.2.0 | April 2026
### Regulatory Reference: Articles 74–99 | Applicable to: All Operators (Providers, Deployers, Importers, Distributors)

---

## Purpose and Scope

This document provides a **Market Surveillance and Regulatory Response Procedure** for organisations subject to the EU AI Act. It covers three areas:

1. **Regulatory readiness** — what documents to have, where to keep them, and how long to retain them
2. **Response to competent authority requests** — how to respond when a national market surveillance authority (MSA) or the EU AI Office contacts you
3. **Corrective action and enforcement** — what to do if an authority finds non-compliance

**Who should use this document:**
- Providers of high-risk AI systems (Annex I/III) — primary audience
- Deployers of high-risk AI systems — secondary audience (deployer-specific obligations noted)
- Importers and distributors — relevant obligations flagged
- GPAI model providers — EU AI Office interaction covered (see also Doc 27)

**Key enforcement dates:**
- Prohibited AI practices: enforceable from **2 February 2025**
- GPAI and general AI Act provisions: enforceable from **2 August 2025**
- High-risk AI (Annex I): enforceable from **2 August 2026**
- All remaining provisions: **2 August 2027**

## Part 1 — Regulatory Landscape: Who Can Come Knocking?

### 1.1 Market Surveillance Authorities

Each EU Member State designates one or more **national market surveillance authorities (MSAs)** responsible for enforcing the AI Act within their territory (Article 74(1)).

**MSA powers include (Articles 74–83):**
- Requesting documents, technical documentation, and access to AI systems
- Inspecting AI systems in operation
- Ordering corrective actions (modifications, restrictions, withdrawal)
- Imposing administrative fines (Articles 99–101)
- Issuing public warnings
- Ordering suspension or recall of AI systems from the market

### 1.2 EU AI Office

The EU AI Office (established under Article 64) has enforcement powers over **GPAI model providers** and coordinates cross-border enforcement for other AI systems.

**EU AI Office powers include:**
- Requesting technical documentation and model access from GPAI providers
- Ordering evaluations and corrective measures for GPAI models with systemic risk (Art. 56)
- Conducting market-wide assessments
- Coordinating with national MSAs on cross-border enforcement

### 1.3 Other Authorities

| Authority | Jurisdiction | Relevant Where |
|---|---|---|
| Data Protection Authorities (DPAs) | GDPR enforcement | AI systems processing personal data |
| Sectoral Regulators (EBA, EMA, EASA, etc.) | Sector-specific law | AI in finance, healthcare, aviation |
| National CSIRT / Cybersecurity Agency (ENISA) | Cybersecurity | AI affecting critical infrastructure |

## Part 4 — Response to Competent Authority Requests

### 4.1 Types of Authority Requests

| Request Type | Authority | Typical Timeframe for Response | Reference |
|---|---|---|---|
| Information request — documents only | MSA | 15 working days (unless urgent) | Art. 74(4) |
| Information request — system access | MSA | Agreed during inspection | Art. 74(4) |
| Corrective action order | MSA | Specified in order (typically 15-30 days) | Art. 79 |
| Urgent safety measure (serious risk) | MSA | Immediate / within 24 hours | Art. 80 |
| Recall or withdrawal order | MSA | Specified in order | Art. 79(5) |
| EU AI Office request (GPAI) | EU AI Office | Specified in request | Art. 56 |
| Administrative fine proceedings | MSA | Per national procedural law | Art. 99 |

### 4.2 First Response Protocol

When a contact is received from a competent authority:

#### Step 1 — Receive and Authenticate (Day 0)

| Action | Owner | Complete? |
|---|---|---|
| Log the contact: date, authority, contact person, request type | Compliance Lead | ☐ |
| Verify the authority's identity and mandate (check national MSA register or EU AI Office contact details) | Legal Counsel | ☐ |
| Confirm request scope in writing (request written confirmation if contact was verbal) | Legal Counsel | ☐ |
| Notify executive team immediately | Compliance Lead | ☐ |
| Engage external legal counsel if not already retained | CEO / Compliance Lead | ☐ |

#### Step 2 — Internal Triage (Days 1–2)

| Action | Owner | Complete? |
|---|---|---|
| Identify all AI systems, documents, and data within scope of request | Technical Lead + Compliance | ☐ |
| Apply legal hold: preserve all relevant documents and logs — do not delete or modify | Legal Counsel | ☐ |
| Assess whether request may indicate: (a) routine surveillance, (b) complaint-based investigation, (c) serious risk or incident investigation | Legal Counsel | ☐ |
| Review all requested documents internally before disclosure | Legal Counsel + Technical Lead | ☐ |
| Identify any legally privileged, trade secret, or confidential third-party information | Legal Counsel | ☐ |

#### Step 3 — Prepare Response (Days 2–14)

| Action | Owner | Complete? |
|---|---|---|
| Compile all requested documents (technical documentation, QMS, RMS, incident reports, logs, etc.) | Technical Lead + Compliance | ☐ |
| Review all documents for completeness and accuracy | Compliance Lead | ☐ |
| Identify and redact genuinely privileged material (with privilege log) | Legal Counsel | ☐ |
| Identify and flag trade secret/confidential commercial information (request confidential treatment) | Legal Counsel | ☐ |
| Prepare covering letter: scope of response, any limitations, requests for extension if needed | Legal Counsel | ☐ |
| Obtain sign-off from Compliance Lead and Legal Counsel before submission | Compliance Lead + Legal | ☐ |

#### Step 4 — Submit Response

| Action | Owner | Complete? |
|---|---|---|
| Submit response via secure channel within requested timeframe | Compliance Lead | ☐ |
| Retain complete copy of everything submitted (including cover letter) | Compliance Lead | ☐ |
| Confirm receipt with authority | Compliance Lead | ☐ |
| Update regulatory interaction log (see Part 4.4) | Compliance Lead | ☐ |

#### Step 5 — Follow-Up

| Action | Owner | Complete? |
|---|---|---|
| Respond to any follow-up questions promptly | Legal Counsel + Technical Lead | ☐ |
| Cooperate with any on-site inspection (see Part 4.3) | All | ☐ |
| If corrective action required: implement per Part 5 | Technical Lead + Compliance | ☐ |
| Post-investigation review: identify root cause and systemic improvements | Compliance Lead | ☐ |

### 4.3 On-Site Inspection Protocol

If the MSA requests an on-site visit or physical/virtual inspection of the AI system:

| Action | Owner | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| Confirm scope, date, and participants in advance | Compliance Lead | Before inspection |
| Brief all staff who may be interviewed: explain rights, no speculation, refer technical questions to designated technical lead | Legal Counsel + HR | Day before |
| Designate a single inspection lead (compliance or legal) who will accompany inspectors at all times | Compliance Lead | Before inspection |
| Prepare a clean, organised documentation room with all relevant documents | Compliance Lead | Before inspection |
| Brief technical lead to demonstrate system capabilities: show what is asked, nothing more | Technical Lead | Before inspection |
| Do not obstruct, mislead, or provide false information to inspectors | All | During inspection |
| Take contemporaneous notes of everything discussed and shown | Inspection Lead | During inspection |
| Request copy of any documents taken or photographed by inspectors | Inspection Lead | During inspection |
| Debrief immediately after inspection: document all topics discussed | All | Same day |

**Key rule:** Cooperate fully and honestly. Obstruction of market surveillance carries severe penalties and will worsen any enforcement outcome.

## Part 5 — Corrective Action and Enforcement Response

### 5.1 Types of Corrective Measures

Under Articles 79–83, MSAs can impose the following corrective measures, in escalating order:

| Measure | Description | Reference | Typical Timeframe |
|---|---|---|---|
| Advisory / guidance | Informal recommendation; not legally binding | Pre-enforcement | Voluntary |
| Corrective action notice | Formal notice to modify, limit, or withdraw; legally binding | Art. 79(1) | 15-30 days to comply |
| Urgent safety measure | Immediate restriction/withdrawal where serious risk present | Art. 80 | Immediate to 48 hours |
| Recall from end users | Requiring operators to take back AI systems already in use | Art. 79(5) | As directed |
| Withdrawal from market | Requiring removal of AI system from EU market | Art. 79(5) | As directed |
| Administrative fine | Financial penalty (see Part 5.3) | Art. 99 | Following proceedings |

### 5.2 Corrective Action Response Procedure

Upon receiving a formal corrective action notice:

| Step | Action | Owner | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Legal hold: preserve all related documents; do not destroy anything | Legal Counsel | Day 0 |
| 2 | Assess notice: understand exactly what is required and by when | Legal Counsel + Technical Lead | Day 0-1 |
| 3 | Consider whether to appeal/contest notice (assess merits and proportionality) | Legal Counsel | Day 1-3 |
| 4 | If not contesting: develop and implement corrective action plan | Technical Lead + Compliance | Per notice deadline |
| 5 | Document all corrective actions taken | Compliance Lead | Ongoing |
| 6 | Report implementation to authority before deadline | Compliance Lead | Per notice deadline |
| 7 | If appealing: file appeal per national procedural law | Legal Counsel | Per applicable deadline |
| 8 | Conduct root-cause analysis and update compliance programme | Compliance Lead | Within 30 days of resolution |

### 5.3 Administrative Fines Reference Table

Article 99 establishes the following maximum fines:

| Violation Category | Maximum Fine | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Violation of prohibited AI practices (Art. 5) | €35 million or 7% of global annual turnover | Art. 99(3) |
| Violation of obligations for high-risk AI systems, transparency, GPAI | €15 million or 3% of global annual turnover | Art. 99(4) |
| Supply of incorrect, incomplete, or misleading information to competent authority or Notified Body | €7.5 million or 1.5% of global annual turnover | Art. 99(5) |
| Violations by SMEs and start-ups | Lower of percentage-based amount or absolute cap | Art. 99(6) |

**Note:** These are **maximum** fines. Actual fines depend on: severity and duration of violation; degree of responsibility; harm caused; cooperation with authorities; prior violations; financial size of the undertaking; and any financial benefits gained from the violation.

### 5.4 Appeals and Judicial Remedies

| Remedy | Description | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Administrative appeal | Challenging MSA decision through national administrative review procedure | National procedural law |
| Judicial review | Challenging MSA decision in national courts | National procedural law |
| CJEU referral | National courts may refer questions of EU law interpretation to the Court of Justice of the EU | Art. 267 TFEU |
| Complaint to EU Commission | If national MSA is systematically failing to enforce | Art. 79 TFEU |
