# 13 -- Authorised Representative Designation (Article 22)

**EU AI Act Reference:** Article 22 | Article 16(k)
**Applies to:** Providers of High-Risk AI Systems not established in the European Union
**Last Updated:** April 2026

---

## Purpose

Article 22 of the EU AI Act requires that providers of high-risk AI systems who are **not established in the European Union** designate an EU-based **Authorised Representative** in writing before placing their system on the EU market or putting it into service.

The Authorised Representative acts as the legal contact point for EU market surveillance authorities and carries specific obligations on behalf of the non-EU provider.

> **Note:** This is distinct from the GDPR Representative requirement under GDPR Article 27. A single entity may hold both roles if appropriately appointed. Always confirm dual-role appointments with legal counsel.

---

## Part 1 -- When Is an Authorised Representative Required?

| Situation | Authorised Representative Required? |
|---|---|
| Provider established in an EU Member State | No |
| Provider established outside the EU placing high-risk AI on the EU market | Yes |
| Provider established outside the EU whose high-risk AI is put into service in the EU | Yes |
| Provider of GPAI model only (not high-risk AI system) | No (but may be required under other legislation) |
| EU-established importer or distributor | No (but has separate obligations under Articles 23-24) |

## Part 4 -- Authorised Representative Obligations (Article 22(3))

By accepting this mandate, the Authorised Representative agrees to perform the following obligations on behalf of the Provider:

### 4.1 Mandatory Obligations

| Obligation | Article | Authorised Representative Confirms |
|---|---|---|
| Register the mandate and contact details in the EU AI Act database | Art. 22(3)(a) | [ ] Confirmed |
| Take necessary action upon receiving a supervisory measure or market surveillance authority request | Art. 22(3)(b) | [ ] Confirmed |
| Cooperate with competent authorities on measures taken in relation to the AI system | Art. 22(3)(c) | [ ] Confirmed |
| Inform the Provider without undue delay of any request from a market surveillance authority | Art. 22(3) | [ ] Confirmed |
| Terminate the mandate and inform authorities if the Provider acts contrary to the EU AI Act | Art. 22(4) | [ ] Confirmed |

### 4.2 Day-to-Day Activities

| Activity | Responsible Party | Communication Channel | SLA |
|---|---|---|---|
| Receiving market surveillance authority communications | Authorised Representative | Email / Registered post | Same business day acknowledgement |
| Forwarding MSA requests to Provider | Authorised Representative | Email | Within 24 hours |
| Coordinating Provider response to MSA | Provider + AR jointly | Email / Call | Per MSA deadline |
| Maintaining mandate registration in EU database | Authorised Representative | EU AI Act database | Before market placement |
| Updating registration upon system changes | Provider informs AR | Email | Within 5 business days |

### 7.2 Termination Obligations

Upon termination, the Authorised Representative must:

- [ ] Notify the relevant EU market surveillance authorities of the termination
- [ ] Update the EU AI Act database registration to remove their details
- [ ] Transfer all relevant documentation and correspondence to the Provider or new AR
