# 22 — Worker Information Notice Template (Article 26(7))

**EU AI Act Reference:** Article 26(7) | Article 26(2) | Article 14(3)
**Applies to:** Deployers of high-risk AI systems in workplace contexts
**Last Updated:** April 2026

## Purpose

Article 26(7) of the EU AI Act requires deployers of high-risk AI systems to inform workers and their representatives **before** deploying a high-risk AI system that affects or is likely to affect the working conditions of those workers. This obligation applies in addition to any information rights under applicable labour law, collective bargaining agreements, and the GDPR.

This document provides:
1. A **notice template** for informing workers about the deployment of a high-risk AI system
2. A **procedure** for fulfilling the Article 26(7) obligation
3. A **tracker** for recording consultations and information events

> **Important:** Article 26(7) requires information to be provided to workers **before** deployment. It does not specify the form or create a right to veto. However, national labour law, works council legislation (e.g. EU European Works Councils Directive), and sector-specific agreements may impose additional obligations including consultation or co-determination rights. Always assess applicable national law before deploying AI systems affecting workers.

1. **Right to human oversight (Art. 14 EU AI Act):** The system is designed to be supervised by humans. Decisions that significantly affect you will be reviewed by [role/name].

3. **GDPR rights:** If the system processes your personal data, you have rights under the GDPR including the right of access (Art. 15), the right to object to profiling (Art. 21), and the right not to be subject to solely automated decisions with legal effects (Art. 22). **Common national law obligations to check:**

- **Germany:** Betriebsverfassungsgesetz (BetrVG) — Works council co-determination rights for monitoring systems (§ 87(1)(6))
- **France:** Labour Code — Information and consultation of the Social and Economic Committee (CSE) before deploying technology monitoring workers (Art. L. 2312-38)
- **Netherlands:** Works Councils Act (WOR) — Works council consent for personnel monitoring systems (Art. 27)
- **Sweden:** Co-Determination Act (MBL) — Employer must consult union before significant changes to operations
- **EU level:** European Works Councils Directive (2009/38/EC) — Information and consultation for multinationals
