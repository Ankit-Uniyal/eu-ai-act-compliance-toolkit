# 12 -- EU Declaration of Conformity Template

**EU AI Act Reference:** Article 47 | Annex V
**Applies to:** Providers of High-Risk AI Systems
**Retention Period:** 10 years after last placement on market (Article 18)
**Last Updated:** April 2026

---

## Purpose

Article 47 of the EU AI Act requires providers of high-risk AI systems to draw up an **EU Declaration of Conformity (DoC)** before placing the system on the market or putting it into service. The DoC is the provider's formal declaration that the AI system meets all applicable requirements of the EU AI Act.

The DoC must be kept up to date and made available to market surveillance authorities on request. Where a system is also subject to other EU harmonisation legislation (e.g. Medical Devices Regulation, Machinery Regulation), a single combined DoC may be drawn up.

> **Important:** This document must be signed by a natural person or legal representative with authority to bind the provider organisation. It is a legally significant declaration. Always have this document reviewed by qualified legal counsel before signing.

---

## When Is a DoC Required?

| Condition | DoC Required? |
|---|---|
| High-risk AI system under Article 6(1) -- safety component of Annex I product | Yes |
| High-risk AI system under Article 6(2) -- Annex III use case | Yes |
| System that has undergone third-party conformity assessment by a Notified Body | Yes |
| Limited-risk AI system (Article 50 only) | No |
| Minimal-risk AI system | No |
| GPAI model only (not deployed as high-risk system) | No |

### Circumstances Requiring a New or Updated Declaration

| Trigger | Action Required |
|---|---|
| Substantial modification to the AI system (Article 25) | New DoC required |
| Change in intended purpose affecting risk classification | New DoC required |
| Notified Body certificate expired or withdrawn | Renewed assessment + new DoC |
| Change in applicable harmonised standards | Review and update DoC |
| New EU harmonisation legislation applies to the system | Update DoC to reference new legislation |
| Provider name or address changes | Update DoC (administrative revision) |
| AI system version update (non-substantial modification) | Assess -- may require DoC update or addendum |
