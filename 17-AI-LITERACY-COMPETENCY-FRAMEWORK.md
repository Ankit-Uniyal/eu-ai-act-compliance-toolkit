# 17 — AI Literacy and Competency Framework

**EU AI Act Reference:** Article 4 | Article 14(3) | Article 26(2)
**Applies to:** All organisations providing or deploying AI systems in the EU
**Last Updated:** April 2026

---

## Purpose

Article 4 of the EU AI Act requires providers and deployers of AI systems to take measures to ensure a sufficient level of AI literacy for their staff, taking into account their technical knowledge, experience, education, and training, as well as the context in which the AI systems are to be used.

Article 14(3) additionally requires that deployers assign human oversight only to persons who have the **necessary competence, training, and authority** to fulfil the oversight role.

This framework provides the structure for meeting these obligations across all roles that interact with AI systems.

## Part 1 — Regulatory Basis and Obligations

### 1.1 Article 4 — AI Literacy Obligation

Article 4 applies to **providers and deployers** and requires:

- Measures to ensure sufficient AI literacy of staff
- Literacy appropriate to role, technical knowledge, experience, and deployment context
- Covers persons dealing with the operation of AI systems AND those using outputs to make decisions

**Key principle:** AI literacy is not a one-size-fits-all requirement. The level and type of literacy required depends on the person's role and the AI systems they interact with.

### 1.2 Article 14(3) — Human Oversight Competency Obligation

Deployers must assign oversight of high-risk AI systems only to persons who have:
- Necessary competence to understand the system's capabilities and limitations
- Training specific to the AI system and its deployment context
- Authority to intervene, override, or suspend the system
- Necessary resources to carry out their oversight tasks

### 1.3 Article 26(2) — Deployer Obligation

Deployers must ensure that persons assigned to human oversight tasks have the competence, training, and authority required by Article 14(3), and provide them with the necessary support.

## Part 2 — Role-Based Competency Framework

### 2.1 Role Categories

| Role Category | Description | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Executive / Board | Strategic decision-makers accountable for AI governance | CEO, Board members, CRO, CDO |
| AI Governance / Compliance | Responsible for regulatory compliance, GRC, and oversight of AI programme | AI Governance Lead, CISO, DPO, Compliance Officer |
| AI Engineering / Technical | Develop, train, maintain, and deploy AI systems | ML Engineers, Data Scientists, AI Architects |
| Product / Business | Define AI use cases, own AI-enabled products and processes | Product Managers, Business Analysts, Operations Leads |
| AI Oversight Persons | Human oversight of high-risk AI system outputs (Article 14) | Recruiters using CV screening AI, doctors using diagnostic AI, loan officers using credit AI |
| End Users / Operators | Day-to-day users of AI systems in a professional context | Customer service reps using AI chatbots, underwriters using risk AI |
| Procurement / Legal | Source, contract, and provide legal advice on AI systems | Legal Counsel, Procurement Officers |

### 2.2 Competency Level Definitions

| Level | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| L1 | Awareness | Understands what AI is, how it is used in the organisation, and what the EU AI Act requires at a high level |
| L2 | Foundation | Understands risk classification, their role-specific obligations, and can identify when to escalate AI-related concerns |
| L3 | Practitioner | Can apply EU AI Act requirements operationally; can conduct assessments, implement controls, or make oversight decisions |
| L4 | Expert | Deep technical and regulatory expertise; can lead assessments, design frameworks, and advise on complex compliance matters |

### 2.3 Competency Requirements by Role

| Role Category | Required Level | Key Competencies Required |
|---|---|---|
| Executive / Board | L2 | AI Act risk tiers; board-level obligations; liability exposure; governance accountability |
| AI Governance / Compliance | L4 | Full Act scope; risk classification; conformity assessment; FRIA; incident reporting; regulatory engagement |
| AI Engineering / Technical | L3 | Technical requirements (Arts. 9-15); data governance; logging; robustness; Annex IV documentation |
| Product / Business | L2-L3 | Use case risk assessment; Article 6(3) exclusions; transparency obligations; deployer responsibilities |
| AI Oversight Persons (High-Risk AI) | L3 | System capabilities and limitations; bias indicators; override procedures; automation bias risk; escalation paths |
| End Users / Operators | L1-L2 | AI disclosure obligations; when to escalate; right to human review; system limitations relevant to their use case |
| Procurement / Legal | L3 | Supply chain obligations; Article 25 provider-becoming triggers; importer/distributor checklists; contractual requirements |
